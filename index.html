What is Censorship?

Restriction of access to information or data. this can be for good, for instance to restrict access to explicit websites, or bad, for example censorship of information opposing a government.

What is surveillence?

Packet sniffers, internet traffic monitoring, keyword / phrases checking, ISPs (Internet Service Providers) keeping logs of websites accessed

Health problems caused by prolonged use of technology

Eye strain caused by looking at a device for too long, RSI (Repetitive strain injury) where repeated movements over prolonged periods of time cause damage, normally fingers, back pain due to poor posture

Methods to prevent these health problems

Using suitable lighting, Keeping good distance from screens, taking regular breaks, arranging desk appropriately, correct posture, taking regular breaks, using an ajustable chair / monitor

What is the data protection act, and what does it do?

The 2018 data protection act is legislation put forwards by the government, which sets out 6 rules:

  • data must be used in a fair, lawfult & transparent way
  • data must only be used for the specified purpose
  • data should be adequate, relevant and not excessive for the specified use
  • data must be accurate and kept up to date
  • data should not be kept for longer than neccesary
  • data should be kept safe & secure

What is the copyright, designs and patents act?

It is an act introduced in 1988, which aims to protect intellectual property (anything someone has created), as well as patents. It criminalises the sharing of copyrighted files without the copyright holder's permission

What is the computer misuse act?

The computer misuse act is a 1990 act introduced to stop hacking and cyber crime. It criminalises:

  • Gaining unauthorised access to a private network or device
  • gaining unauthorised access to a network or device in order to commit a crime, like stealing data or destroying the network
  • unauthorised modification of computer material, such as deleting or chainging files.
